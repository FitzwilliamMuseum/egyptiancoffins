Pop Ups

With the generous support of the Arts and Humanities Impact Fund (University of Cambridge), a small team from the Fitzwilliam Egyptian Coffins project have been able to pilot a Pop-Up Museum in Wisbech. The aim of this is to bring our cutting-edge, interdisciplinary research into ancient Egyptian coffins into culturally underserved communities who might not otherwise have access to the research we are doing. We have selected Wisbech, located about 1 hour 20 minutes from central Cambridge by car, given that it is reported to be one of the most deprived towns not only in Cambridgeshire, but the whole of the United Kingdom. According to the 2011 Census, 35.1% have no formal qualifications and 19.1% have below the national standard average for literacy levels. With a population of around 35,000 people, there is also a growing rate of migrants, predominantly from Lithuania, Latvia and Poland. But, what is a Pop-Up Museum? And how do we engage audiences using this type of platform?
